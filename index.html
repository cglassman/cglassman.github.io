Intro

I'm a data analyst with a passion for making a positive impact in the world. My journey began with a degree in physics from the University of California, Santa Barbara, where I also explored the exciting field of astropysics. It was at a conference for undergraduate women in physics (CUWIP) that I first learned about stereotype threat and imposter syndrome, and I was inspired to help others who may have faced similar challenges. That's why I founded the Undergraduate Diversity in Physics (UDIP) organization, which aimed to create a welcoming and inclusive environment for people from all backgrounds.

My hard work and dedication were recognized with several awards, including the Dean's Award for Outstanding Senior, the Physics Department Award of Academic Honors, and the Physics Department Chair's Senior Appreciation Award. I was even given the opportunity to speak about imposter syndrome at CUWIP, a testament to the impact that UDIP had made.

After being encouraged by a professor, I decided to pursue a graduate degree in medical physics, where I discovered my love for data analytics and healthcare. My desire to help others, combined with my expertise in data analysis, makes me a valuable asset in the field of healthcare and beyond.
